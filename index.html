About Me

Hi, I'm Hakam. I build hardware for High Performance Computing applications. I consequently write a lot of very low level C and C++ to both tune and benchmark the performance of my custom hardware. I have also recently taken an interest in building applications that aim to expose security vulnerabilities in hardware.

I'm currently an undergraduate student at McMaster University studying Computer Engineering. During my co-op, I interned at The Six Semiconductor, where I was involved in the design and bring-up of an LPDDR5x PHY. During this period, I was also actively involved in research under Dr. Kazem Cheshmi in optimizing SPMM kernels. I have also been supervised by Dr. Nicola Nicolici where I developed some understanding of Digital Communication Networks and Communication Protocols.
